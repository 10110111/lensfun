How to upload

Please select a tarball (.tar.gz) or a ZIP file from your computer that contains the RAW pictures. Note that my internet uplink can only handle up to 3 MByte/sec, so the transfer may take a long time. Your upload may contain RAWs for many different lenses, but only one camera.

Important: By uploading files here, you thereby grant me the right to permanently store them on my computer and use them for generating calibration data. This data is then published under the Creative Commons Attribution-Share Alike 3.0 license. I will not publish or give away your images or your email address without your explicit permission.

How should test shots look like?

Test shots need to be taken at a distance of at least 3 metres. The pictures must contain one straight line, running from one end of the picture to the other, close to the top border of the picture. Ideally, it contains a second straight line 1/3 of the image height from the top.

The picture on the left is a good example. The numbered marks follow the two lines that I mentioned. I recommend that you use a modern building and take pictures of it. Tilting or rotating the camera is allowed. Make really sharp pictures. Switch off all corrections (some cameras do this even for RAWs).

If you calibrate a zoom lens, take pictures at at least five different focal lengths. Always include the extreme values.

TCA and vignetting

The more corrections are possible, the better. As for TCA (chromatic aberration), I can maybe use the same pictures that you uploaded for distortion above. Anyway, they need to contain sharp high-contrast edges over the whole image, see the photo on the right.

As for taking pictures for vignetting correction, see my lens calibration tutorial.

About EXIF data …

I need to know lens model, focal length, and aperture for each shot. Mostly, this is already included in the RAW. But sometimes, it isn’t. Or the lens data is incorrect because you used a converter etc. In these cases, you may include the data into the file name. For example, you may rename a raw file to

    Canon_EF_24-70mm_f__2.8L_USM--35mm--2.8.CR2

In the lens name, a single underscore “_” represents a space “ ”, and double underscore “__” represents a slash “/”.

This is, however, only absolutely required if the EXIF data is plain wrong (converters etc). Otherwise, you will be pointed to a web page where you can add any missing data.

What happens after the upload?

I will send you an email with the calibration data, together with instruction for how to install them locally. At the same time, I will send this data to the LensFun project.
